Reflection 1A

Throughout the early stages of the ARBUS 400 Capsim competition I have shown an extremely high level of initiative. I dedicated multiple hours almost every day before the practice rounds to playing through the tutorial numerous times to learn more about the intricacies of the game. After I got a good feel for how Capsim worked, I continued using the tutorial for testing purposes. My group started to formulate a strategy during the practice rounds so I used the tutorial to test different potential tactics and to better judge the impact of each unique variable in the simulation. This due diligence allowed me to prescribe what I determined to be the correct course of action with a high degree of confidence.

For each input our group had to make I was able to critically analyze the situation and contribute to an informed decision based on the extensive amount of work I had put into this project. At this point in the simulation it is impossible to draw definite conclusions about the effectiveness of our decisions, it is however worth noting that we have not needed the emergency loan, and there have been no surprises thus far. In the first year we expected that we would lose money so I insisted that we borrow the maximum amount allowed in long term debt, as well as issuing the maximum amount of shares. These decisions prevented us from needing the emergency loan after the first year of the simulation.
